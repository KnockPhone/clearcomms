Built on standards, not opinions

ClearComms rewrites the same way every time, so you can explain any change to a colleague, client or sign-off lead.

01Plain English, reading age 9 to 11

We aim below most people's top reading level so the message lands first time, mirroring plain-English guidance used across the NHS and GOV.UK.

02People-first, non-stigmatising

We name the person before the condition and avoid blame words, drawing on inclusive-language guidance from groups such as Mind and the Samaritans media guidelines.

03Accessible by default

CamelCase hashtags, meaningful link text, sentence case over block capitals and sparing emoji, in line with WCAG and platform guidance.

04Calm, clear punctuation

No shouting in capitals and no em dashes. Calmer punctuation reads better aloud and feels more trustworthy.

Questions, answered

Does ClearComms replace a human reviewer?

No. It is assistive. It speeds up the first pass and catches the easy-to-miss things, but a person should always have the final say before publishing.

Where does the guidance come from?

From established UK plain-English, inclusive-language and accessibility standards, including guidance reflected across the NHS and GOV.UK, the Samaritans media guidelines on suicide, and WCAG for accessibility.

Is my content private?

Your drafts and checks belong to your organisation and are not shared with other accounts. When AI review is switched on, posts are sent to the connected Claude model to produce the rewrite.

Can I match our own tone of voice?

Yes. Brand profiles let you set your tone notes, banned words and a reading-age target, and ClearComms applies them on every check.

Which platforms does it support?

LinkedIn, X, Instagram and Facebook, each with the right character limits and preview cut-offs.
